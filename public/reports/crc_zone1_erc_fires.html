Method & caveats

Fire growth. Cumulative acreage per ICS-209 incident situation report (ICS-209-PLUS v2.0 research dataset, 1999–2020). Where reports skip days, the acreage gain is spread evenly across the gap. Final acreages shown match the fire-matching manifest: Mayberry 25,385; Alkali 19,569; Dead Dog 17,731.

ERC. FEMS daily NFDR summaries (fuel model X — Brush) for the FDRA's stations — Lodore (50104), Calico (50108), Dragon Road (51407), Hunter Creek (51406), Pinto (51402) — downloaded from the FEMS climatology API. SIG daily ERC is the mean of stations reporting that day. Unlike the earlier UCR Middle Elevation report, which had to substitute gridMET ERC-G, this report uses station ERC on the FDRA's own fuel-model scale, so thresholds here are the FDRA's own percentiles: Mar–Oct 2005–2024 90th percentile = 95.1, 97th = 100.1 (4,900 station-mean days). FEMS returns no NFDR records for these stations before 2005-01-01 (1999–2004 queried, empty), so pre-2005 fires appear without ERC.

Statistics. “Significant growth day” = daily gain ≥2% of final fire size (min 100 ac). ρ is Spearman rank correlation between daily ERC and daily growth over each fire's active days; group differences (significant vs. other days) use a Mann–Whitney U normal approximation. Fires with fewer than 4 situation reports, stub timelines, or no station ERC for their year are shown for context and excluded from pooled statistics. This is a small, non-random sample of memorable fires — results describe these incidents, not a general model.
