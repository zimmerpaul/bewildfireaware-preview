What it shows

No fire in this FDRA supports day-by-day ERC comparison. The matched fires here are too small or too briefly documented for daily analysis — none has 4 or more days of usable growth reporting — so they are shown as timeline facts only and no correlation or pooled statistics are claimed.

This is a small, non-random sample — 2 fires chosen for a pocket card because they were memorable — so these numbers describe these incidents, not a general model for GMUG Uncompahgre FDRA.

Method & caveats

Fire growth. Cumulative acreage per ICS-209 incident situation report (ICS-209-PLUS v2.0 research dataset, 1999–2020); Love: rough timeline assembled from WFIGS and news reports, not daily sitreps. Where reports skip days, the acreage gain is spread evenly across the gap. Final acreages shown match the fire-matching manifest: Love 30; Sawmill 12.

ERC. FEMS daily NFDR summaries (fuel model Y — Timber) for the FDRA's stations — Jacks Canyon (52409), Sanborn Park (53804), Carpenter Ridge (53808) — downloaded from the FEMS climatology API. SIG daily ERC is the mean of stations reporting that day. Unlike the earlier UCR Middle Elevation report, which had to substitute gridMET ERC-G, this report uses station ERC on the FDRA's own fuel-model scale, so thresholds here are the FDRA's own percentiles: Mar–Oct 2005–2024 90th percentile = 56.4, 97th = 63.2 (4,900 station-mean days). FEMS returns no NFDR records for these stations before 2005-01-01 (1999–2004 queried, empty), so pre-2005 fires appear without ERC.

Statistics. “Significant growth day” = daily gain ≥2% of final fire size (min 100 ac). ρ is Spearman rank correlation between daily ERC and daily growth over each fire's active days; group differences (significant vs. other days) use a Mann–Whitney U normal approximation. Fires with fewer than 4 situation reports, stub timelines, or no station ERC for their year are shown for context and excluded from pooled statistics. This is a small, non-random sample of memorable fires — results describe these incidents, not a general model.

Sources: ICS-209-PLUS v2.0 (figshare 19858927; Nature Sci. Data 2023) · FEMS (fems.fs2c.usda.gov) NFDR daily summaries · NWCG pocket cards · WFIGS and contemporary news coverage where noted. Generated July 2026 for bewildfireaware.com.
