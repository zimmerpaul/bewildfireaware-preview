What it shows

Only one fire here supports day-by-day ERC comparison. Buttermilk (2018) is the single fire in this FDRA with a usable daily growth timeline paired with station ERC, so no pooled statistics are shown. Its 3 significant growth days averaged the 85th percentile of the FDRA's Mar–Oct ERC record, versus the 87th on its other active days.

The biggest runs cluster on high-ERC days. Buttermilk's biggest run (+211 ac on Jul 27, 2018) came at the 89th percentile of ERC.

Within-fire, day-to-day correlation varies (Buttermilk ρ = -0.47). ERC measures fuel dryness, not wind — the day-to-day trigger for the largest runs is often wind on top of dry fuels, which is exactly the pocket card's point that wind is not part of the ERC calculation. This is a small, non-random sample — 1 fire chosen for a pocket card because they were memorable — so these numbers describe these incidents, not a general model for GMUG Montrose FDRA.

Method & caveats

Fire growth. Cumulative acreage per ICS-209 incident situation report (ICS-209-PLUS v2.0 research dataset, 1999–2020). Where reports skip days, the acreage gain is spread evenly across the gap. Final acreages shown match the fire-matching manifest: Buttermilk 747.

ERC. FEMS daily NFDR summaries (fuel model Y — Timber) for the FDRA's stations — Cottonwood Basin (53805), Jay (52704) — downloaded from the FEMS climatology API. SIG daily ERC is the mean of stations reporting that day. Unlike the earlier UCR Middle Elevation report, which had to substitute gridMET ERC-G, this report uses station ERC on the FDRA's own fuel-model scale, so thresholds here are the FDRA's own percentiles: Mar–Oct 2005–2024 90th percentile = 62.3, 97th = 68.9 (4,900 station-mean days). FEMS returns no NFDR records for these stations before 2005-01-01 (1999–2004 queried, empty), so pre-2005 fires appear without ERC.

Statistics. “Significant growth day” = daily gain ≥2% of final fire size (min 100 ac). ρ is Spearman rank correlation between daily ERC and daily growth over each fire's active days; group differences (significant vs. other days) use a Mann–Whitney U normal approximation. Fires with fewer than 4 situation reports, stub timelines, or no station ERC for their year are shown for context and excluded from pooled statistics. This is a small, non-random sample of memorable fires — results describe these incidents, not a general model.

Sources: ICS-209-PLUS v2.0 (figshare 19858927; Nature Sci. Data 2023) · FEMS (fems.fs2c.usda.gov) NFDR daily summaries · NWCG pocket cards · WFIGS and contemporary news coverage where noted. Generated July 2026 for bewildfireaware.com.
