Craig Zone 2 FDRA — ERC & Largest Fires on Record

Energy Release Component vs. daily fire growth for the largest fires on record in the Craig Zone 2 FDRA (no pocket card exists for this FDRA) — Pine Tree (2017), Divide (2018), Iron (2023), the three largest fires by final acres inside the FDRA polygon in federal records, 2005–2024. Daily fire sizes come from ICS-209 incident situation reports; daily ERC is the FEMS station record for the FDRA's SIG — Great Divide (50106), Dry Lake (50207), Deep Creek (51510) — fuel model Y — Timber, averaged across stations, 2005–2024.

No pocket card exists for this FDRA — the fires shown are the largest fires on record in this FDRA (the three largest by final acres with a point of origin inside the FDRA polygon, federal fire records 2005–2024), not pocket-card “fires to remember.”

Pine Tree (2017), Iron (2023): too little daily data for correlation claims (stub or <4 situation reports) — shown as timeline facts only, excluded from pooled statistics.

What it shows

Only one fire here supports day-by-day ERC comparison. Divide (2018) is the single fire in this FDRA with a usable daily growth timeline paired with station ERC, so no pooled statistics are shown. Its 8 significant growth days averaged the 97th percentile of the FDRA's Mar–Oct ERC record, versus the 95th on its other active days.

The biggest runs cluster on high-ERC days. Divide's biggest run (+6,401 ac on Jul 7, 2018) came at the 97th percentile of ERC.

Within-fire, day-to-day correlation varies (Divide ρ = 0.05). ERC measures fuel dryness, not wind — the day-to-day trigger for the largest runs is often wind on top of dry fuels, which is worth remembering because wind is not part of the ERC calculation. This is a small, size-selected sample — the 3 largest fires on record in this FDRA (no pocket card exists) — so these numbers describe these incidents, not a general model for Craig Zone 2 FDRA.

Method & caveats

Fire growth. Cumulative acreage per ICS-209 incident situation report (ICS-209-PLUS v2.0 research dataset, 1999–2020); Iron: rough timeline assembled from WFIGS and news reports, not daily sitreps. Where reports skip days, the acreage gain is spread evenly across the gap. Final acreages shown match the fire-matching manifest: Pine Tree 4,735; Divide 19,955; Iron 7,392.

ERC. FEMS daily NFDR summaries (fuel model Y — Timber) for the FDRA's stations — Great Divide (50106), Dry Lake (50207), Deep Creek (51510) — downloaded from the FEMS climatology API. SIG daily ERC is the mean of stations reporting that day. Unlike the earlier UCR Middle Elevation report, which had to substitute gridMET ERC-G, this report uses station ERC on the FDRA's own fuel-model scale, so thresholds here are the FDRA's own percentiles: Mar–Oct 2005–2024 90th percentile = 51.0, 97th = 56.3 (4,900 station-mean days). FEMS returns no NFDR records for these stations before 2005-01-01 (1999–2004 queried, empty), so pre-2005 fires appear without ERC.

Statistics. “Significant growth day” = daily gain ≥2% of final fire size (min 100 ac). ρ is Spearman rank correlation between daily ERC and daily growth over each fire's active days; group differences (significant vs. other days) use a Mann–Whitney U normal approximation. Fires with fewer than 4 situation reports, stub timelines, or no station ERC for their year are shown for context and excluded from pooled statistics. This is a small, non-random sample of memorable fires — results describe these incidents, not a general model.

Sources: ICS-209-PLUS v2.0 (figshare 19858927; Nature Sci. Data 2023) · FEMS (fems.fs2c.usda.gov) NFDR daily summaries · NWCG pocket cards · WFIGS and contemporary news coverage where noted. Generated July 2026 for bewildfireaware.com.
