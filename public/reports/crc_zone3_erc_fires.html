What it shows

Significant growth days sit high on this FDRA's own ERC scale. Pooled across the 3 fires with usable daily data (Beaver Creek (2016), Ryan (2018), East Troublesome (2020)), significant growth days averaged the 87th percentile of station ERC, versus the 71st on other active days (28 significant days out of 136 active fire days). At or above the 90th percentile, 13 of 33 active days produced significant growth (39%); below the 75th percentile it was 2 of 56 (4%).

The biggest runs cluster on high-ERC days. Beaver Creek's biggest run (+5,168 ac on Jun 23, 2016) came at the 77th percentile of ERC; Ryan's biggest run (+12,491 ac on Sep 23, 2018) came at the 96th percentile of ERC; East Troublesome's biggest run (+134,073 ac on Oct 22, 2020) came at the 87th percentile of ERC.

Within-fire, day-to-day correlation varies (Beaver Creek ρ = 0.50, Ryan ρ = 0.29, East Troublesome ρ = 0.78). ERC measures fuel dryness, not wind — the day-to-day trigger for the largest runs is often wind on top of dry fuels, which is worth remembering because wind is not part of the ERC calculation. This is a small, size-selected sample — the 3 largest fires on record in this FDRA (no pocket card exists) — so these numbers describe these incidents, not a general model for Craig Zone 3 FDRA.

Method & caveats

Fire growth. Cumulative acreage per ICS-209 incident situation report (ICS-209-PLUS v2.0 research dataset, 1999–2020). Where reports skip days, the acreage gain is spread evenly across the gap. Final acreages shown match the fire-matching manifest: Beaver Creek 38,380; Ryan 28,585; East Troublesome 193,812.

ERC. FEMS daily NFDR summaries (fuel model Z — Slash/Blowdown) for the FDRA's stations — Dry Lake (50207), Gunsight (50404), Sandstone (482012), Sawmill Park (482105) — downloaded from the FEMS climatology API. SIG daily ERC is the mean of stations reporting that day. Unlike the earlier UCR Middle Elevation report, which had to substitute gridMET ERC-G, this report uses station ERC on the FDRA's own fuel-model scale, so thresholds here are the FDRA's own percentiles: Mar–Oct 2005–2024 90th percentile = 92.6, 97th = 101.1 (4,900 station-mean days). FEMS returns no NFDR records for these stations before 2005-01-01 (1999–2004 queried, empty), so pre-2005 fires appear without ERC.

Statistics. “Significant growth day” = daily gain ≥2% of final fire size (min 100 ac). ρ is Spearman rank correlation between daily ERC and daily growth over each fire's active days; group differences (significant vs. other days) use a Mann–Whitney U normal approximation. Fires with fewer than 4 situation reports, stub timelines, or no station ERC for their year are shown for context and excluded from pooled statistics. This is a small, non-random sample of memorable fires — results describe these incidents, not a general model.
